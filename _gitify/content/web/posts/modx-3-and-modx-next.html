We've been regularly posting about the future of MODX. From providing links to Jason Coward's articles on Medium, to a summary of what was known and ideas from the community about what the future might, or should, look like. 

One thing that was failed to properly communicate is the difference between MODX 3 and MODX Next. These are not new terms, but people seem to think they're the same while they're not, and even here on MODX.today we've made that mistake. 

MODX 3 is the next logical evolution for MODX Revolution. It's a release that will have breaking changes from MODX Revolution 2.x, but unnecessary breaking changes will be avoided. It's a chance to upgrade MODX to the next level by removing legacy and making things better at the expense of breaking (some) backwards compatibility with MODX 2. 

MODX Next is a completely reimagined version of MODX. It's unlikely to share a lot of code with the current versions of MODX, might be completely different, and is not necessarily going to be version 4 or even 5. It might just be version 1 of a completely different product. 

Personally I'd love to see many of the improvements originally slated for MODX Next to be included in MODX 3, but at the same time we wont be able of adding on to the current code indefinitely, and MODX Next provides a blank slate for that.
